Doctrinal Statement
Six essential beliefs affirmed by all churches in the Sou discípulo de Jesus - Boca Network
Sou discípulo de Jesus - Boca Network
These six doctrinal essentials form the theological foundation of our network. They are the non-negotiable convictions shared across all member churches — regardless of denomination, worship style, or secondary distinctives. Unity on these truths is required for network participation and spiritual covering.
1
The Authority of Scripture
The Bible is the inspired, infallible Word of God — the supreme authority for faith and practice in the life of every believer and every church. Scripture alone is the final rule for doctrine, correction, and training in righteousness.
2 Timóteo 3:16–17 · 2 Pedro 1:20–21 · Salmo 119:105
2
The Triune God
There is one God, eternally existing in three persons — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit — equal in nature, distinct in person, and unified in purpose. God alone is Creator, Sustainer, and Lord of all things.
Mateus 28:19 · Deuteronômio 6:4 · 2 Coríntios 13:14
3
The Person & Work of Christ
Jesus Christ is fully God and fully man, born of a virgin, lived a sinless life, died on the cross as the atoning sacrifice for sin, rose bodily from the dead, ascended to the Father, and will return in glory to judge the living and the dead.
João 1:1,14 · Isaías 53:5 · 1 Coríntios 15:3–4 · Atos 1:11
4
Salvation by Grace through Faith
Salvation is a gift of God's grace received through faith in Jesus Christ alone — not by human works or merit — resulting in regeneration, forgiveness of sins, justification before God, and the promise of eternal life.
Efésios 2:8–9 · Romanos 3:23–24 · João 3:16 · Tito 3:5
5
The Holy Spirit & New Birth
Every believer is born again by the Holy Spirit, who indwells, seals, and empowers the Christian for holy living, spiritual gifts, and witness. The Spirit glorifies Christ and leads the church in truth, mission, and prayer.
João 3:5–8 · Romanos 8:9–11 · Atos 1:8 · 1 Coríntios 12:4–7
6
The Great Commission & the Church
The church is the body of Christ, called to worship, disciple, and multiply. Every local church and micro-church expression is commissioned to make disciples of all nations through baptism, teaching, fellowship, and Spirit-led mission.
Mateus 28:18–20 · Atos 2:42–47 · Efésios 4:11–13
Support Local Churches

We encourage every disciple to understand what it means to be the Church and to support all local churches that align with the essential doctrines. Local churches are not all the same — differences exist — but the essence of Christ must be present in each one. Pray, reflect, seek understanding, and walk the path of being a disciple and making disciples.
